Our lawyers regularly advise non-banking financial companies (NBFCs) and prospective NBFCs on regulations prescribed by the Reserve Bank of India. We assist clients with registration and licensing considerations, governance norms, and on enquiries and investigations. Our lawyers regularly advise fintech companies, payment system operators, and other technology-led financial businesses on regulatory, contractual, and corporate matters.

Our team is experienced in managing parallel and multi forum proceedings, including criminal complaints, ED linked investigations, and writ petitions before constitutional courts, ensuring a coordinated and strategic defence across all fronts. We also support regulated entities such as scheduled commercial banks & NBFCs on commercial arrangements, contracting frameworks and adherence to legal framework governing such entities.

Our practice in the NBFC, fintech, and payments space is shaped by a deep understanding of the rapid technological and regulatory transformation occurring in India’s financial sector. As new business models emerge and digital financial services expand, companies face increasing scrutiny from regulators and heightened expectations around governance, data protection, and consumer protection. We help clients navigate this evolving landscape by offering clear, practical advice on licensing, operational compliance, outsourcing arrangements, and product structuring. Our team works closely with fintech innovators, payment aggregators, lenders, and financial institutions to ensure that their business models align with regulatory requirements while retaining commercial flexibility.

In contentious and enforcement matters, we provide comprehensive support across multiple forums, ensuring that clients maintain a consistent and strategic defence. Our experience includes handling investigations by enforcement agencies, responding to regulatory notices, and representing clients in writ proceedings before High Courts. We also advise on commercial arrangements such as co-lending partnerships, payment system integrations, and technology service agreements, ensuring that contractual frameworks reflect regulatory expectations and industry best practices. With a blend of legal expertise, sectoral insight, and strategic thinking, our practice helps clients operate confidently in one of India’s most dynamic and closely regulated sectors.
